<p><strong>lnkmx 6-Week App Roadmap</strong></p><p><strong>Planning window: March 9, 2026 - April 19, 2026</strong></p><p>Goal: launch the Q2 monetization pivot without breaking the strengths already present in the repo - the page builder, Dashboard V2, Business Zones CRM, invoicing/documents, analytics, SEO/SSR, PWA shell, and Capacitor mobile wrappers.</p><p>Repo basis used for this roadmap: README, PLATFORM_SNAPSHOT, product roadmap/tech debt docs, dependency-cruiser rules, tier hooks and monetization screens, fintech service, Zone screens, and manifest/service worker behavior.</p><p><strong>Current Repo Baseline</strong></p><p>- The repo already ships a broad surface area: 28+ block page builder, public pages, Dashboard V2, Business Zones (deals, contacts, tasks, invoices, documents, products, events, automations), SEO/SSR, i18n, PWA assets, and Capacitor shells.</p><p>- Several CRM depth features are already present in code, including multi-pipeline support, custom contact/deal fields, Excel exports, invoice PDF export, and a Zone command palette/global search.</p><p>- The clearest near-term gap is monetization alignment: docs call for Identity/Starter/Pro with 7% and 1% fee handling, while current hooks and billing UI still model free/pro/business and the fintech service still carries a 5% default take-rate.</p><p>- Billing/account UX is unfinished in places: Account Settings still contains TODO placeholders for billing history and password change, and the payment service is still described as a skeleton.</p><p>- PWA/offline support is basic today: the service worker caches a small static set and images, but not the deeper read-only CRM workflow described in Q2 plans.</p><p>- Zone analytics exists, but it is still a summary dashboard rather than a full operating/reporting surface for finance, funnel trends, and team throughput.</p><p><strong>Architecture Constraints</strong></p><p>- Protect Supabase RLS and zone isolation on every billing, reporting, and CRM change.</p><p>- Respect dependency-cruiser boundaries between core/shared code and UI/data layers.</p><p>- Keep bundle growth under control; the app already lazy-loads shell pieces and zone screens for performance.</p><p>- Treat Edge Function changes as security-sensitive because several public endpoints rely on internal auth checks instead of JWT verification.</p><p>- Keep quality gates green: typecheck, lint, unit tests, E2E flows, and dependency analysis.</p><p><strong>Milestones</strong></p><p>- Milestone 1 - End of Week 2: monetization foundation live across tier rules, payment flows, fee handling, and billing surfaces.</p><p>- Milestone 2 - End of Week 4: business-ready CRM polish with stronger reporting, accessible mobile UX, and
